CCPA Compliance

California Consumer Privacy Act rights fully respected. Know your data, control your privacy, exercise your rights with confidence.

Last Updated: January 26, 2025 | Effective Date: January 1, 2020

Your California Privacy Rights

BankCSVConverter is committed to transparency and respecting the privacy rights of California residents under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). This page explains your rights and how to exercise them.

We Do NOT Sell Your Personal Information

BankCSVConverter has never sold personal information and commits to never selling your data in the future.

Your Rights Under CCPA

As a California resident, you have specific rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Know

Request disclosure of what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell (we don't sell data).

Right to Delete

Request deletion of personal information we collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.

Right to Opt-Out

Opt-out of the sale of personal information (not applicable as we don't sell data).

Right to Non-Discrimination

Exercise your privacy rights without receiving discriminatory treatment.

How We Use Your Information

Business Purposes

  • Providing bank statement conversion services
  • Processing transactions and managing accounts
  • Communicating about our services
  • Improving and developing our services
  • Detecting security incidents and protecting against fraud
  • Debugging and repairing errors
  • Complying with legal obligations

Sources of Personal Information

  • Directly from you: When you create an account or use our services
  • Automatically: Through cookies and similar technologies
  • Service providers: Payment processors, analytics providers

Information Sharing & Disclosure

We Share Information Only When Necessary

We may share your personal information with:

  • Service Providers: Companies that help us operate our business (hosting, analytics, support)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or to protect rights and safety
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, sale, or acquisition
  • With Your Consent: When you explicitly agree to sharing

Verification Process

To protect your privacy and security, we will verify your identity before processing requests. We may ask you to:

  • Provide information that matches our records
  • Confirm your email address
  • Sign a declaration under penalty of perjury (for certain requests)

Response Timeline

  1. Acknowledgment: Within 10 business days of receiving your request
  2. Response: Within 45 days (may be extended by 45 additional days if necessary)
  3. Deletion: Completed within 45 days of verification

Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent to make requests on your behalf. The agent must provide:

  • Written permission signed by you
  • Verification of their identity
  • Direct confirmation from you about the authorization (we may contact you)

California "Shine the Light" Law

California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits residents to request information about disclosure of personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. We do not share personal information with third parties for their direct marketing purposes.
