International Bank Support: Now Supporting 1000+ Banks Worldwide

By Alex Chen, Head of Operations December 15, 2024

1. Our Global Expansion Journey

When we launched BankCSVConverter in 2019, we supported just 50 US banks. Today, we're proud to serve customers in over 80 countries with support for their local banking institutions. This growth has been driven by our users' needs and our commitment to making financial data accessible worldwide.

The Challenge of International Banking

Supporting international banks isn't just about adding more names to a list. Each country has unique:

  • Banking regulations: Different data privacy laws and compliance requirements
  • Statement formats: Varying layouts, data structures, and information types
  • Language variations: Statements in dozens of languages with different character sets
  • Date and number formats: DD/MM/YYYY vs MM/DD/YYYY, decimal separators, etc.
  • Currency symbols: Proper handling of various currency formats and symbols

Our Approach to Global Support

We've developed a sophisticated parsing engine that:

  1. Automatically detects bank and country of origin
  2. Applies region-specific parsing rules
  3. Handles multiple languages and character sets
  4. Normalizes data to standard formats
  5. Maintains compliance with local regulations
Fun Fact: Our AI now recognizes bank statements in 47 different languages, from Arabic to Vietnamese!

4. Multi-Language Statement Support

One of our biggest technical achievements is supporting bank statements in multiple languages and scripts. Our system now handles:

Supported Languages and Scripts

Latin Script: English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, etc. Cyrillic: Russian, Ukrainian, Bulgarian, Serbian Arabic: Arabic, Farsi, Urdu Asian Scripts: Chinese (Simplified/Traditional), Japanese, Korean, Thai, Vietnamese Other: Greek, Hebrew, Hindi (Devanagari)

Language Detection and Processing

Our advanced language processing system:

  1. Auto-detects language: Using AI to identify the statement language
  2. Applies language rules: Different parsing logic for each language
  3. Translates key fields: Optionally translate transaction descriptions
  4. Preserves original text: Maintains source language for reference
  5. Handles mixed languages: Common in international banking
Pro Tip: You can export your converted statements with both original and translated descriptions for easier accounting reconciliation.

Character Encoding Support

We support all major character encodings:

  • UTF-8 (default and recommended)
  • UTF-16 for complex scripts
  • ISO-8859 variants for legacy systems
  • Windows-1252 for older European statements
  • GB2312/GBK for Chinese banks
  • Shift-JIS for Japanese banks
