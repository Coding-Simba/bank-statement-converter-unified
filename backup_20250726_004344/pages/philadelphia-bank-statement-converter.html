Philadelphia: America's Historic Financial Center

Philadelphia holds a unique place in American financial history as the birthplace of the nation's banking system. The city that hosted the First and Second Banks of the United States continues to be a major financial hub in the Northeast corridor.

Banking Heritage

From the establishment of the First Bank of the United States in 1791 to becoming home to the Philadelphia Stock Exchange (America's oldest stock exchange), Philadelphia has been at the forefront of American finance. Today, the city maintains its financial prominence with major banks like Citizens Bank headquartered here and significant operations from national banks.

Modern Financial Hub

Philadelphia's financial district in Center City houses numerous banks, investment firms, and fintech companies. The city's banking landscape reflects its economic diversity, with major national banks maintaining significant operations alongside strong regional players. Philadelphia's economy is anchored by its "eds and meds" sector - education and healthcare - which includes world-renowned institutions like the University of Pennsylvania, Temple University, and the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia.
